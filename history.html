Before going into detail about CryptoCurrencies, let us have a look at its predeccessors: Money throught the Ages

When money hadn't yet been invented, the barter system was used. Introduced by Mesopotamian tribes, the history of bartering dates all the way back to 9000 BC. This trading method did not involve usage of money, and relied solely on exchanging goods and services for other services and goods in return.

For example: A farmer could exchange a bushel of wheat for other groceries.

Barter system had many difficulties which were faced by the people.

  • Lack of double coincidence of wants
  • Lack of a common measurement value
  • Difficulty in the storage of the value
  • Difficulty in making future/contractual payments

To overcome the problems of Barter Trade, early humans started devising a system of payments & exchange. Money was developed according to needs & requirements, with the Main aim to remove the shortcomings of the barter system.

From Barter to Bitcoin:

With the progress of human civilization, commodity money changed into metallic money. In the beginning pieces of gold and silver were used as money but later these were replaced with token coins. The world’s first official currency was minted in 600 B.C in the ancient kingdom of Lydia. These coins were made of electrum.

People, however, found it inconvenient & dangerous to carry gold and silver coins from place to place. This led to the invention of paper currency. The first paper currency, Jiaozi was used in China, many years before Europeans started using banknotes.

After paper came plastic money such as credit and debit cards. The concept of Credit card was first introduced in 1946 by John Biggins, a Brooklyn banker. He named it 'Charge-It'. Soon after this, cryptocurrencies came along, and it looks like they're here to stay.
