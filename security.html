Many investers are scared that their crypto will be hacked. What they don't know, is that CryptoCurrencies deploy various security measures to stay ahead of malicious parties. Here we will discuss its security issues and advantages.

Issue #1:
The is an issue of inheritance (anonymity). All crypto transactions are anonymous. While this can be advantageous to honest users, hackers can use this anonymity to make illegal transactions without ever getting caught. Also, funds of passed relatives are inaccesible.

For example: Deceased Michael's father spent three years trying to find out how many Bitcoins his son had, but, without knowing all the adresses, he was unable to locate/use the lost peices of Bitcoin.

Advantage #1:
Cryptocurrency payments are settled immediately without the involvement of any third party. This removes the need of trusting any third party, and also paves the way for even more and even stronger cryptography, which in turn improves security and reduces the risk of being hacked.

Without anyone else needed, the cost of making the transaction itself is greatly reduced. The transaction is also completed in a fraction of the time required for traditional ones.

Issue #2:
Cryptocurrency does not improve existing trust. All of us know that it is difficult for us to trust unknown strangers we have never met before on anonymous computer networks with our huge Bitcoin transactions.To a certain level, we can already do that. This trust means that even the security of the system could be compromised, for we don't know who is at tyhe other end.

This trust, of course, depends on many factors about the individuals, culture, and environmental when interacting with other accounts on Bitcoin. Due to its anonymous nature, Bitcoin removes this implicit trust that already exists and replaces it with anonymous financial incentives.

Advantage #2:
Cryptocurrencies prevent inflation. Inflation causes the value of any currency to decrease overtime. This can be prevented by specifying how many units of the currency will ever exist. Almost every cryptocurrency is released with a fixed maximum amout specified in its source code. Thus, the currency's value increases overtime, keeping the market up and preventing inflation.

For example: There are only 21 million Bitcoins are released in the world, although many of them haven't been mined yet.

Issue #3:
Crypto is decentralized: it is not governed by any upper authority, etc.

Bitcoin prides itself in being a decentralized peer-to-peer exchange network without authorizing middlemen or governments to oversee this business.

This lack of central authority in cryptocurrency transactions is considered a threat to governments, but can also be advantageous because decentralization means that there is no single point of failure, and the comprmisation of a single block in the blockchain will not break open the others.

Advantage #3:
Blockchains are immutable. Yes, immutable. You may be confused, for all the previous issue pointers point out various vunerabilities in the blockchain, right? No. Blockchains are made up of complex mathematical puzzles, like the 'proof of work' algorithm, among others. This makes them immutable.

As for the previous vunerabilities, they can many a times be found in the apps that handle the blockchain, not the blockchain itself. Some huge cryptocurrency hacks were targeted on the apps that handle the chain, not the chain itself, for the chain is immutable. A few examples of huge hacks are

  • Bithumb: $30 Million
  • Coinrail: $37.2 Million
  • BitGrail: $195 Million
  • Coincheck: $534 Million
